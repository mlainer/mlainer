Short biography

I graduated in 2010 with a B.Sc and 2012 with a M.Sc degree from the Ludwig-Maximilian-University (Munich), where I majored in physics and meteorology. In the Master thesis I studied persistent contrails based on numerical simulations with a cloud-resolving LES model. From August 2013 to June 2017 I performed my PhD studies in Physics at the Institute of Applied Physics (IAP) of the University of Bern. The main topic of my thesis was to investigate middle atmospheric dynamics based on water vapor measurements from ground-based microwave radiometers. After my PhD I investigated water vapor trends and 2-day wave dynamics in the middle atmosphere in the course of a one year post-doctoral position at the University of Bern. Since summer 2018 I am working at MeteoSwiss as a scientific collaborator within the radar, satellite and nowcasting division.
